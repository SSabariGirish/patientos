Let's get this sorted.

It is completely okay. You've done the right thing by checking.

First, take a deep breath. Mistakes with medication happen all the time. The best thing to do right now is simply get a quick professional opinion.

Here is what to do next:

1. Have a quick chat with NHS 111

Dial 111 on your phone. It's free, available 24/7, and you'll speak to trained staff who deal with this every day. Just let them know what you took and roughly when.

2. Keep the boxes handy

While you're on the phone, grab the packaging for the medicines. The person on the line might ask you to read the exact ingredients off the back to help them advise you.

3. When to dial 999 instead:

If YOU took the medicine:
Please don't wait. Dial 999 for an ambulance immediately if you are struggling to breathe, feeling faint, or experiencing severe chest or stomach pain.

If you are helping someone else:
Dial 999 immediately if they are unconscious, having a seizure, or having difficulty breathing.

A quick note: Please don't try to force anyone to vomit the medicines unless a medical professional on the phone specifically tells you to. Just make the call and follow their lead.
